🫧 The Float Test

On Day 8+, drop a tablespoon of starter into room-temperature water. If it floats, your starter is ready to bake with. If it sinks, keep feeding for another day or two.

Understanding Ratios

Ratios like 1:2:2 mean: 1 part starter : 2 parts flour : 2 parts water (all by weight).

As your starter matures, you increase the ratio (e.g. 1:4:4, 1:10:10) to slow fermentation and reduce sourness.

Storing Your Mature Starter

Feed your starter, let it sit at room temperature for 2 hours, then refrigerate. Feed once a week if kept cold. For very active starters, feed twice daily at room temp using a 1:7:7 or 1:10:10 ratio.
